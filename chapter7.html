chapter 7
Social Responsibility

Learn more


The Association

  1. Every member who wishes to invite the members to his wedding must give at least one month notice to the Association.

  2. The Association shall be duly represented by at least thirty members except in the case of where we have multiple weddings on the same day.

  3. The members would contribute a minimum of one thousand naira only.

  4. The member is entitled to a purse of fifty thousand naira only.

    The member/celebrant

  1. The celebrant shall provide an adequate sitting arrangement.

  2. The members in attendance are to be entertained with a cooler of rice,a pot of soup, adequate semovita or akpu, two cartons of beer and two cartons of soft drinks.

  3. He shall equally provide sufficient souvenirs for the members present.

Death of a Member

  1. The death of a member SHALL be related to the Association by anybody with the said information as soon as practical.

  2. The Association shall inaugurate a committee to work with the bereaved family and other stakeholders to ensure our maximum participation.

  3. Every member will contribute a minimum of one thousand naira.

  4. The Association SHALL be fully represented; hence, every member is encouraged to attend.

  5. The association would give the family about one hundred thousand naira as a condolence purse.

  6. The family is expected to provide an organized sitting arrangement and refreshments if practicable.

Death of a Members Parent

  1. A bereaved member shall inform the association of the death of his parent or a person in loco parentis (in place of a parent) and the necessary funeral arrangements.

  2. The Association shall commiserate with a bereaved member through calls, texts, tributes and other mediums applicable.

  3. The bereaved member may intimate the association whether he will welcome the attendance of the members or not.

  4. The association SHALL attend the funeral and be fully represented by a minimum of twenty members if the member(s) so elect(s) and if given a reasonable notice.

  5. Every member is expected to donate a minimum of five hundred naira.

  6. The member is entitled to a purse of fifteen thousand naira.

  7. The bereaved member shall have in place an organized sittingarrangement for the Association.

  8. The members in attendance shall be entertained with one cooler of rice,two crates of beer and one crate of soft drink.

Dues

  • Every member shall pay annual dues of one thousand two hundred naira only.

  • The Association might decide to solicit for more funds or dues for specific purposes as shall be determined by the General Assembly.

Special Clause

  • Whatever money that accrues to the body through dues, fines and other incomes shall be deposited into the Associations bank account within seven days.

  • The Financial Secretary shall prepare a comprehensive list of indebted members and announce the same in every meetings of the Association.

  • Every unnecessary expense shall be avoided.

Defamation of the Association

This association prides in being seen as a reputable group hence any member who defames, maligns or brings to disrepute the Association shall be fined five thousand naira and an apology letter tendered to the body and documented or the member risks being suspended until the payment and the said apology is tendered.

Misconduct

Every member is expected to conduct themselves in the most ethical standard. Any grievance or disaffection between members in relation to the association shall be reported and discussed in the General Assembly. The decision of the General Assembly shall be binding.

Any member that exhibits any form of misconduct or in an unprofessional conduct shall be fined with two thousand naira and an apology letter tendered and documented by the body or risk being suspended until the payment and the said apology is tendered.


The idea of the Alumni Association was conceived when it was considered necessary to create an organization that would embrace different generations of the University. The body was expected to play a dominant role in the life of the University by reflecting the views of its graduates.
