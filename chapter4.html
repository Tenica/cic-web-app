Chapter4
Governance

Learn more

The Associations SHALL be divided into two organs:

The General Assembly:

This comprises of every registered member. It shall be the highest decision making organ of the association.

Executive Council

The day to day affairs of the association SHALL be managed by the executives who shall be elected by the general assembly.

The following executives shall make up the executive council members:

  1. President

  2. Vice President

  3. Secretary

  4. Assistant Secretary

  5. Financial Secretary

  6. Treasurer

  7. Public Relations Executive (P.R.O) 1

  8. Public Relation Executive (P.R.O) 2

  9. Director of Social (D.O.S) 1

  10. Director of Social (D.O.S) 2

  11. Provosts 1 and 2

President

  1. The association shall have a President as the Chief Executive.

  2. He shall through the Public Relation Executive summon the meetings of the association.

  3. He shall Preside over the meetings of the association.

  4. He shall have the veto power on issues save the election of officers, the general house cannot reach consensus through an election.

  5. He shall authorize in writing, and be one of the signatories in the disbursement of the association fund.

  6. He shall represent the association in any emergency where the presence of the association is needed.

  7. The president shall have the disciplinary power over any person but in all the discharge of his functions he must have recourse to natural justice, equity and good conscience and must be subject to public opinion.

Vice President

  1. He shall assist the President in the discharge of the functions as stated in Section (1).

  2. He shall perform any other duties which the President may from time to time assign to him.

  3. He shall act in absence of the president.

  4. He shall be in charge of the welfare of the members in other words he will be the chairman of the bodys Welfare Committee.

Secretary

  1. The secretary shall be in charge of every secretarial work.

  2. He shall keep the minutes of the association.

  3. He shall keep record and handle all correspondences of the association.

  4. He shall write down the amount realized at the end of every meeting.

  5. He shall Preside the meeting in the absence of the President and Vice President.

Assistant Secretary

  1. He shall in conjunction with the P.R.O, on the Presidents instruction issue public notice for the associations meetings.

  2. He shall do the work of the secretary in the absence of the secretary.

  3. He shall take record of the proceedings of the executive meetings,meetings with Old Boys Association and the patrons etc.

  4. He shall take attendance of the members in the meeting.

  5. He shall perform other ancillary secretarial duties as the Secretary may assign to him from time to time

Financial Secretary

  1. The financial Secretary shall keep a financial book of the association and record in the financial book all monies accruing to the association.

  2. He shall hand over to the treasurer all monies collected at every meeting or occasion any time not less than seven (7) days and obtain receipt for same.

  3. He shall keep record of the associations expenditure.

  4. He shall prepare,read and submit to the general house a report of the associations financial position, whenever occasion demands but especially at the last meeting of the year.

  5. He shall continuously report to the Secretary of over-due debts owed the association for proper action.

  6. He shall Preside the meeting in the absence of the President, Vice President, Secretary or Assistant Secretary.

Treasurer

  1. The treasurer shall keep the entire associations money.

  2. He shall in conjunction with the President and the Financial Secretary operate the associations bank account.

  3. Pursuant to sub paragraph (II) above, all the associations money exceeding two thousand naira only (N2, 000) must be lodged into the said account.

  4. The treasurer shall keep an imprest account of not more than N2, 000 (two thousand naira).

  5. He shall submit his books annually to the auditors for inspection if need be.

  6. He shall not pay any bill without a written order or voucher duly signed by the Financial Secretary and counter signed by the President.

  7. He shall Preside the meeting in the absence of the President, Vice President, Secretary, Assistant Secretary or Financial Secretary.

Public Relation Officer I(P.R.O I)

  1. The P.R.O I shall make public the activities of the association to members as may be required by the association.

  2. He shall promote the image of the association formally as may be required by the association.

  3. He shall Preside the meeting in the absence of the President, Vice President, Secretary, Assistant Secretary, Financial Secretary or Treasurer.


Public Relation Officer II (P.R.O II)

The P.R.O II shall assist the P.R.O I in the discharge of duties as stated for the public relations officerPRO1

Director of Social I (D.O.S I)

  1. The Director of Socials shall co-ordinate and executes all social activities of the association including and not limited to sports, outings, visitations, retreats, Picnic, get together, seminars, symposia etc.

  2. He shall take charge of all the social instrument of the association.

  3. He shall Preside the meeting in the absence of the President, Vice President, Secretary, Assistant Secretary, Financial Secretary, Treasurer or Public Relations Officer.


    4. Director of Social II (D.O.S II)

    1. He shall assist the D.O.S I in the discharge of duties as stipulated in Paragraph (I) sub paragraphs (I), (II) and (III).

    2. A committee shall be called to assist the D.O.S if need be.

Provost

The Association shall have two (2) provost; Provost I &II.

  1. They shall take note of all the late comers and collect their fines as stipulated by the constitution.

  2. They shall maintain orderliness and discipline during meeting and other social functions of the association.

  3. They shall collect appropriate fines from defaulters and send them to the financial Secretary before the end of the meeting.

  4. They shall take note of the venue of meetings and sitting arrangement of members.

  5. They are empowered to take any reasonable step to recover fines from defaulters.

  6. He shall Preside the meeting in the absence of the President, Vice President, Secretary, Assistant Secretary, Financial Secretary, Treasurer, Public Relations Officer or Director Of Socials.

Tenure of Executives

The tenure of the executive members shall be three years single tenure after which the member shall not be eligible for election into any office for duration of three years PROVIDED that if he is elected to serve out the unexpired tenure of an executive member whose office had been vacated under Section 14(3) the rule on single tenure SHALL not apply

Committees

  1. This Association whenever necessary can operate through an adhoc committee for the proper attainment of its objectives.

  2. Where there is need for a committee to be instituted, the President shall call for nominations from the General Assembly.

  3. Only active and qualified members should be nominated.

  4. Where the members nominated exceed the required number needed, there shall be an election.

  5. The members with the highest votes in the election shall be deemed elected.

  6. After such election, the relevant powers including terms of reference or finance required of the constituted committee to function optimally shall be delegated or allotted to them.

  7. The committee shall at every point be accountable to the association by frequent delivery of reports.

  8. The committee shall not act beyond the powers delegated to them and if need be, recourse must be made to the association for approval.

  9. For emphasis, the association has absolute sovereignty and so has the power to amend, alter, ratify or reject any decision made by the committee.

  10. At the end of their assignment, a detailed account must be laid on the floor of the meeting for either approval or rejection.

  11. An account duly presented and accepted by the General Assembly brings to end the lifespan of such body.

Patrons

The association should when necessary appoint deserving members of the public as their patrons. Such patrons may be appointed from the senior members of the Old Boys Association of College of the Immaculate Conception Enugu. They shall help with the running of the association making sure that the ideals of this body are not dragged to the mud. Their advice should be sought in any aspect especially areas the constitution did not provide for.


The idea of the Alumni Association was conceived when it was considered necessary to create an organization that would embrace different generations of the University. The body was expected to play a dominant role in the life of the University by reflecting the views of its graduates.
